# AI Contribution Policy

PwnForge is a security-critical tool: it stores flags, writeups, and credentials for teams actively competing, and it's self-hosted by people who trust it not to leak. Maintaining that trust requires deep intent behind every change, not just code that happens to compile. There is currently one maintainer. That maintainer's time is the project's scarcest resource.

We recognize AI-assisted tools (Claude, Copilot, ChatGPT, etc.) as legitimate, useful aids for development. But the same tools make it easy to generate contributions the submitter doesn't fully understand, which shifts the cost of that gap onto the maintainer reviewing it. This policy exists to keep that cost proportional to what a small team can actually carry.

## 1. Human accountability

**The human contributor is the sole party responsible for the contribution.**

If your Pull Request includes AI-generated code, documentation, or comments:

- You must fully understand every line you submit.
- You must be able to explain the *why* behind the implementation during review, not just what it does.
- You are responsible for its long-term maintenance, including bugs the tool that wrote it didn't anticipate.

"The AI generated it and it works" is never an acceptable answer to a reviewer's question. If a maintainer suspects you don't understand your own PR, it will be closed without further discussion. Autonomous AI agents may not open Pull Requests directly: a human submits, and that human is accountable for what's in it.

## 2. Disclosure

If AI meaningfully assisted your contribution (beyond simple autocomplete), disclose it with an `Assisted-by:` trailer on the commit, alongside your `Signed-off-by:` (see [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md)):

```md
Assisted-by: Claude Code
Signed-off-by: Your Name <your.email@example.com>
```

`Assisted-by:` names the tool, not necessarily a model version. It is never a substitute for `Signed-off-by:`. Only a human can certify the DCO.

## 3. Intentionality

PwnForge is not a testing ground for AI experimentation. PRs generated by running a tool over the codebase looking for "improvements," with no prior context or alignment with the project, will be closed.

- **No shotgun refactoring.** Don't submit AI-suggested wide-scale cleanup unless a maintainer specifically asked for it.
- **Design first for anything non-trivial.** Open an issue or discussion before a PR with significant AI-generated logic. Arriving unannounced with an architectural change that doesn't match current design principles or [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) policies gets closed, not debated in the PR thread.
- **Quality over quantity.** One thoughtful PR beats ten AI-assisted fixes for issues that don't really exist.
- **Never submit unverified exploit code or PoCs as if they were tested.** This is specific to what PwnForge is: if an AI tool generated a payload, exploit chain, or proof-of-concept, you must have actually run and confirmed it before describing it as working. An unverified AI-generated exploit presented as functional is worse than no contribution at all on a tool built for people who will rely on it during a live competition.

## 4. Copyright and legal

By contributing, you represent that:

1. You have the right to submit the contribution under this project's license (AGPL v3).
2. The contribution doesn't violate a third party's intellectual property rights.
3. If AI was used, the output doesn't violate that AI provider's terms of service and doesn't include regurgitated code from a dependency whose license is incompatible with AGPL v3.

If you can't guarantee the provenance of AI-generated code, don't submit it.

## 5. Prohibited uses

These result in immediate closure and, on repeat, a block from the repository:

- **Automated PR descriptions.** We want to hear from you why the change matters, not AI prose describing it in the vaguest terms that technically apply.
- **Unvetted boilerplate.** Large AI-generated blocks that haven't been trimmed to what's actually necessary.
- **Hallucinated features or bugs.** PRs "fixing" something that was never broken, based on an AI tool's incorrect assumptions about how PwnForge works.

## 6. Enforcement

A maintainer may close a PR that looks like a low-effort AI contribution without providing a detailed technical critique. That critique is exactly the maintainer time this policy exists to protect. Repeated violations can result in a block from the repository. Unlike Ghostty (below), we're not maintaining a public denouncement list: that's infrastructure worth its overhead at their scale, not at a single-maintainer project's.
